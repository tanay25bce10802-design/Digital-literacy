# Social Media Checklist for College Students

Navigating social media as a college student requires balancing personal expression with building a positive personal brand for your future career. Use this checklist to keep your online presence responsible and professional.

## ✅ The Do's

* [ ] **Do review your privacy settings regularly:** Ensure your personal accounts are set to private and that you understand who can see your content, tag you, and share your posts.
* [ ] **Do build a positive professional brand:** Use platforms like LinkedIn to highlight your academic achievements, volunteer work, internships, and extracurricular involvement.
* [ ] **Do apply the "Future Employer" rule:** Before posting a photo or comment, ask yourself if you would be comfortable with a hiring manager, professor, or scholarship committee seeing it.
* [ ] **Do engage constructively:** Follow industry leaders in your field of study, participate in academic or career-focused groups, and support your peers' achievements.
* [ ] **Do manage your screen time:** Set limits on your app usage to ensure social media doesn't negatively impact your study habits, sleep schedule, or mental health.

---

## ❌ The Don'ts

* [ ] **Don't post evidence of illegal or rule-breaking behavior:** Avoid posting photos or videos involving underage drinking, drug use, or violations of your university's code of conduct.
* [ ] **Don't vent about professors, peers, or employers:** Complaining about a difficult professor or a bad shift at work leaves a permanent, searchable negative footprint. Keep venting offline.
* [ ] **Don't overshare personal security details:** Never post your dorm room number, daily class schedule, phone number, or real-time location, as this poses a major safety risk.
* [ ] **Don't engage in cyberbullying or public arguments:** Avoid getting dragged into heated debates, trolling, or making derogatory comments. Always maintain digital etiquette.
* [ ] **Don't rely solely on social media for research or news:** Verify facts using credible sources or your university library before sharing information or using it in your academic work.
