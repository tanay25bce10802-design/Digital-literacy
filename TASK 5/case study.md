Case Study: The "Collect Request" UPI Scam
The Crime
Unified Payments Interface (UPI) fraud, specifically the "Collect Request" scam, is a form of digital financial theft where fraudsters exploit the speed of real-time payments and a lack of user technical literacy to drain bank accounts.

How it Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

The Bait: A fraudster poses as a buyer on a marketplace (like OLX) or a customer care executive. They express interest in an item or offer a "refund."

The Hook: They claim they are sending money but ask the victim to "authorize" the receipt.

The Trigger: Instead of sending money, the scammer sends a "Collect Request" via a UPI app (e.g., Google Pay, PhonePe).

The Deception: The victim, often rushed or distracted, sees a notification. The scammer tells them, "Just enter your PIN to receive the payment."

The Theft: Once the victim enters their UPI PIN, they have unknowingly authorized a debit rather than a credit. The funds are instantly transferred to the scammer’s account.

Target Demographic
While anyone can be a target, scammers frequently zero in on senior citizens (who may be less tech-savvy), first-time digital users in rural areas, and students or young adults selling items online.

Consequences
The immediate impact is total financial loss of the authorized amount, which is often difficult to recover due to the instant nature of UPI. Secondary consequences include identity compromise, as scammers may use captured details for further phishing, and a significant loss of trust in digital banking systems. Legal recourse involves filing a report at cybercrime.gov.in, though recovery depends on how quickly the bank is notified to freeze the recipient's account.
