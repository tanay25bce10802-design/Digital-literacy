Stay Safe Online: A Cybercrime Prevention Checklist for Students
Navigating the digital world safely is essential, especially with the heavy reliance on smartphones, online payments, and public networks on campus. Follow this checklist to protect your personal and financial data.

🔐 General Online Safety & Privacy
1. Secure Your Accounts with 2FA: Always enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on your email, WhatsApp, and social media accounts. This adds an extra layer of security even if someone guesses your password.

2. Beware of "Freebie" Phishing Links: Do not click on unverified WhatsApp or SMS links claiming to offer "free OTT subscriptions," "free mobile recharges," or "guaranteed internships." These are common traps to steal your data.

3. Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks: Avoid making transactions or logging into banking portals while connected to free college campus or cafe Wi-Fi. If necessary, use a trusted VPN.

4. Restrict Social Media Privacy: Keep your social media profiles private. Do not accept friend requests from strangers or share sensitive personal details (like your exact live location or ID cards) online to prevent identity theft and stalking.

5. Stick to Official App Stores: Only download applications from the official Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid downloading cracked software or third-party APKs, as they frequently contain malware or ransomware.

6. Keep Devices Updated: Regularly update your phone and laptop's operating system and apps. These updates often contain critical security patches.

💸 UPI & Financial Safety
7. Remember the Golden Rule of UPI: You only need to enter your UPI PIN to SEND money, never to RECEIVE money. If someone asks you to scan a QR code or enter your PIN to "process your refund" or "receive a prize," it is a scam.

8. Verify the Receiver’s Identity: Always double-check the registered name that appears on your screen after scanning a merchant QR code or entering a phone number before you hit 'Pay'.
